Are you concerned about your child? Do they need support because of a loss, family or school issue, challenging emotions or learning difficulties?

Working with children and families over the last 20 years has given me an in-depth understanding of child and adolescent development. It can be daunting to figure out why your child or teen is having challenges and what kind of support they need. My role is to help them gain resilience and skills to navigate their world, and to help you more effectively support your child.

With younger children, I rely on play therapy and cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) in my work to help build coping skills, empathy, and better social skills for children in a developmentally-appropriate manner. Play is the primary language for children, so I encourage children to express and explore thoughts and feelings through various forms of play including sand tray and art therapy. I also use CBT to teach skills that can be utilized outside of our sessions, helping children recognize their own emotions, notice and critically evaluate their thoughts, and use adaptive behavioral strategies, mindfulness, and other self-soothing techniques to remain grounded in difficult situations or when having big emotions.

With adolescents, I focus on CBT, expressive and talk therapy. Therapy reinforces the message that your child's thoughts and feelings are important, and that talking about issues and thoughtful problem solving are important life tools. Young people also often appreciate the chance to explore their relationships, goals and perspectives with an adult outside their family.

Effective child and adolescent therapy relies on your involvement, so my goal is to partner with you to support your child. Collaboration with teachers and other adults can also have a profound impact, so I encourage phone or in-person consultations when appropriate.

It's important that you find a therapist who is the right fit for your child. I'm glad to answer any questions you have about my practice, therapy or how to find help. Please contact me to set up an introductory phone call.
